Combining ElevenLabs voice generation with Descript's transcript-based editing creates a workflow that's faster than either tool alone. Here's how to set it up.

## Why Combine ElevenLabs and Descript?

ElevenLabs is a best-in-class voice generator. Descript is a best-in-class audio and video editor with transcript-based editing. Each does one thing extremely well, and they complement each other in ways that neither tool's native workflow quite achieves alone.

<InfoCallout type="info" title="The core problem this workflow solves">
ElevenLabs generates excellent voice audio, but editing AI-generated audio in a standard audio editor is painful — you're editing waveforms that correspond to specific words, and changes require regeneration. Descript's transcript editor means you can make text-based edits and see them reflected in the audio. When combined with ElevenLabs' ability to regenerate specific sentences, content editing stays at the text level throughout production.
</InfoCallout>

## The Step-by-Step Combined Workflow

1. **Script finalisation in ElevenLabs Projects.** Write your final script in ElevenLabs Projects. Generate all sections, listen through, and regenerate any sentences that don't sound right. Export the full audio as a high-quality MP3 or WAV file.

2. **Import into Descript.** Create a new project in Descript and import your ElevenLabs-generated audio file. Descript will transcribe the audio automatically, creating an editable transcript synchronised to the audio timeline.

3. **Structural editing in Descript.** Use Descript's transcript editor to make structural changes: remove sections, reorder paragraphs, cut filler content, or trim pacing. Deleting text in the transcript removes the corresponding audio automatically — significantly faster than waveform editing.

4. **Identify sentences needing regeneration.** As you edit, flag any sentences where the AI delivery doesn't match what you want — wrong emphasis, awkward pacing, or sentences you've revised. Note the exact text of each flagged sentence.

5. **Regenerate flagged sentences in ElevenLabs.** Return to ElevenLabs Projects and regenerate only the specific sentences you flagged. Download each as an individual audio file.

6. **Swap in regenerated clips.** In Descript, use the **Replace Clip** feature to swap the old versions with the regenerated ones at the correct timeline positions.

7. **Final export.** From Descript, export your final edited audio (or video if you've added visuals) at your required format and quality settings.

<InfoCallout type="tip" title="Save time on regeneration">
Only flag sentences that genuinely need fixing before you go back to ElevenLabs. Batching all your regeneration requests into a single return session is significantly more efficient than switching between tools sentence by sentence.
</InfoCallout>

## Best Use Cases for This Combined Workflow

### Podcast Production

The most common use case. Generate intro, outro, and scripted segments in ElevenLabs. Import into Descript. Add any recorded interview audio or real recordings. Use Descript's transcript tools to clean up and structure the episode. Export as the final produced file. This workflow reduces episode production time significantly compared to traditional audio editing approaches.

### YouTube Narration

Generate narration in ElevenLabs Projects, import to Descript, then use Descript's timeline to sync narration against your B-roll footage or screen recordings. Make timing adjustments at the text level rather than by cutting waveforms. Export as video from Descript with the final synced audio baked in.

### Course Module Production

Generate all module audio in ElevenLabs. Import into Descript for quality review and structural editing. Add any screen recordings or slide presentations in Descript's video timeline. Export individual lesson files or a combined module video.

## Why Not Just Use Descript's Built-in AI Voice?

Descript has its own AI voice generation capability through the **Overdub** feature. If you're wondering why you'd use ElevenLabs at all when Descript already includes AI voice, the answer comes down to quality and flexibility.

<EditorialNote type="note" title="Overdub vs ElevenLabs — honest comparison">
Descript's Overdub feature is designed specifically for filling in corrections to recordings of your own voice — regenerating short sections where you misspoke or made edits. It's excellent for that specific use case. As a standalone voice generation tool for producing full narration from text, it produces results that are noticeably less natural than ElevenLabs' models. The voice options are also more limited.
</EditorialNote>
