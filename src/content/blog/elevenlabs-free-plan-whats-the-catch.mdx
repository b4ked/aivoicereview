10,000 free characters per month sounds generous. Here's what you can actually do with the ElevenLabs free tier — and the real limitations worth knowing before you commit.

## What the Free Plan Actually Includes

The ElevenLabs free plan provides 10,000 characters per month with no credit card required. In practical audio terms, that's approximately 1,500–1,800 words, or roughly 10–12 minutes of finished audio at normal speaking pace.

Free plan users get:

- Access to the full pre-made voice library (over 1,000 voices across accents, genders, ages, and use cases)
- Up to 3 custom voices
- Instant Voice Cloning at a basic level
- Audio downloads included
- Commercial use with attribution

<InfoCallout type="tip" title="Same interface as paid plans">
The interface on the free plan is identical to paid plans — you're not working in a cut-down version of the product. You have the same generation controls, the same voice settings panel, and the same download options. The limits are in volume and access to specific features, not in the product experience itself.
</InfoCallout>

## The Real Limitations

**What works well**

- No credit card required to sign up
- Full voice library access (1,000+ voices)
- Commercial use permitted (with attribution)
- Good enough to genuinely evaluate voice quality
- Same interface as paid plans

**Real limitations**

- 10,000 characters/month runs out fast in real production
- No Projects feature for long-form content
- No API access
- Lower generation quality than paid tiers
- Voice cloning quality ceiling is lower than paid plans
- Attribution required for commercial use

### Volume

The most significant limitation is volume. 10,000 characters per month disappears quickly once you're using ElevenLabs for real content production.

| Content Type | Approx. Characters | Free Plan Monthly Budget |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| 5-minute video script | ~3,500–4,000 chars | ~2–3 scripts/month (no regeneration) |
| 10-minute video script | ~7,500–9,000 chars | ~1 script/month |
| 30-min podcast episode | ~22,000+ chars | Not feasible |

<EditorialNote type="warning">
These estimates assume zero regeneration. Every time you regenerate a passage, it costs the same characters again. In practice, the free plan runs out even faster than the table suggests.
</EditorialNote>

### No Projects Feature

The Projects feature — ElevenLabs' long-form narration editor — is not available on the free plan. This matters for anything longer than a few paragraphs, because Projects is what makes long-form content practically manageable. Without it, you're working with individual text snippets and manual stitching.

### No API Access

API access is not available on the free plan. If you're evaluating ElevenLabs for integration into a product or automation workflow, you'll need at minimum the Starter plan to test with the API.

### Lower Generation Quality

The free plan uses different model settings than paid plans — the voice output is noticeably less polished. The gap is most audible on longer passages and on voices with more subtle characteristics. If the free plan already sounds good for your use case, paid quality is meaningfully better.

## Commercial Use Rules on the Free Plan

ElevenLabs permits commercial use on the free plan, but with an attribution requirement. Generated audio used in public-facing content must include attribution to ElevenLabs as the generation source — in practice, disclosing that the voice was AI-generated by ElevenLabs.

<InfoCallout type="info">
For most personal and hobbyist use, attribution is a non-issue. For professional content where you'd prefer not to disclose the AI voice tool explicitly, it's a meaningful constraint. Paid plans from Starter upward remove the attribution requirement.
</InfoCallout>

<EditorialNote type="warning" title="Read the Terms for commercial products">
Using free-plan audio in commercial products you sell — courses, audiobooks, app integrations — technically falls under ElevenLabs' commercial use rules. ElevenLabs has not been aggressive about enforcement, but if commercial use matters to you, starting on a paid plan is cleaner.
</EditorialNote>

## Voice Cloning on the Free Plan

Instant Voice Cloning is available on the free plan, limited to 3 custom voices. The quality ceiling on free-plan cloning is lower than on paid plans — ElevenLabs uses a faster, less accurate model for free users.

In practical terms:

- Free-plan cloning is good enough to **understand whether the technology meets your needs**
- It is not good enough for **public-facing content where the clone needs to be convincing**
- Think of it as proof-of-concept capability, not production capability

<InfoCallout type="tip">
If the clone quality on the free plan already impresses you, the paid version will be meaningfully better. If it already falls short for your use case, the paid version addresses most of those gaps.
</InfoCallout>

<EditorialNote type="note" title="Professional Voice Cloning requires paid plans">
Professional Voice Cloning — which uses 30+ minutes of source audio and produces significantly higher-quality results — is not available on the free plan at any level.
</EditorialNote>

## When the Free Plan Is Enough (and When It Isn't)

The free plan is genuinely useful in a few specific scenarios:

1. You're **evaluating** whether ElevenLabs' voice quality justifies the price
2. You have **very occasional audio needs** — one or two short pieces per month
3. You're **building something in development** and need to test the product before committing budget

The free plan is not sufficient for:

- Any **regular content production schedule**
- Anything requiring **long-form narration editing** (Projects)
- Any **product integration** requiring API access
- Professional commercial use where **attribution disclosure isn't appropriate**

<InfoCallout type="tip" title="The easiest upgrade decision in this category">
The step up to Starter ($5/month) is one of the clearest value decisions in AI tools — it triples the character allowance, adds API access, removes attribution requirements, and costs less than a coffee. If you've used the free plan and found ElevenLabs useful, upgrading to Starter is almost always worth it immediately.
</InfoCallout>

The real decision point is **Starter vs Creator ($22/month)** — that's where the Projects feature, improved quality settings, and meaningful character volume open up.
